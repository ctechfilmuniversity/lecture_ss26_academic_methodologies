#### Submission - Paper Description:   

In the Paper I want to explore how cuteness shapes the current revival of Frutiger Aero, which is a digital aesthetic from the early 2000s which is experiencing an unexpected comeback. 
This paper draws on two key concepts: Antoni Gaudí's idea that "originality consists in returning to the origin" and the concept of hauntology, wich describes how "dead" cultural phenomena return, feeling simultaneously familiar and strange. This Paper will argue that cuteness functions as a reactivation mechanism for forgotten digital aesthetics.
Frutiger Aero is characterized by whimsical (visual and sonic) elements like soft and bubbly water-like sounds, bubbles, glossy surfaces and bright organic forms. These are characteristics that Joshua Paul Dale, a pioneer in the emerging field of cuteness research, defined as cute. In his book "Irresistible: How Cuteness Wired our Brains and Conquered the World" he explains how the aesthetics of cuteness spread around the globe. He also gives a revealing look at how our most powerful psychological impulses have remade global style and culture. These cute characteristics trigger strong emotional and psychological responses, making the aesthetic feel inviting and accessible rather than uncanny. 
With this Paper I want to observe a broader pattern: first the frutiger aero trend which I already mentioned and second the cyberdeck trend, which originated in the academic hacker culture of the 1950s–1980s at institutions like MIT which is currently being revived. The term cyberdeck is shaped by William Gibsons novel „Neuromancer“ (1984), in which a cyberdeck is described as a portable computer used by hackers to infiltrate virtual worlds. This shaped the image of the cyberdeck as a tool for digital rebels.
Currently, this trend is being driven primarily by young women who bring their own cute and whimsical aesthetic to the practice. Alongside this, tutorials, workshops and a general rise of "Tech Girlies" on social media imply that cuteness is not just a visual style but a mechanism that makes technology emotionally accessible to new audiences. 

In this case, Frutiger Aero serves as the primary case study through which the observations are examined.
