# Task 03.02 - Storylines

#### I. Complete the following three sentences:

1. ***I am examining/working on/writing about ...* → What am I writing about? (name your research topic)**
    1. I am writing about the contemporary hyperfeminine cyberdeck movement as a cultural and aesthetic counter-response to the increasing exclusivity of mainstream tech culture…
2. ***... because I want to find out/understand/show ...* → What do I want to know? (name your research question)**
    1. ...because I want to show how this community, through style, DIY practice, and online visibility, reclaims technology for groups historically excluded from tech spaces, at a moment when AI development and Big Tech rhetoric are actively narrowing who belongs in tech…
3. ***... in order to understand/determine/verify...* → Why do I want to gain this knowledge? (state your research goal)**
    1. ..in order to understand how aesthetic and community-driven DIY practices can challenge dominant, exclusionary structures in tech culture.

#### II. Reformulate what you have written:

1. **Rephrase 3. by finishing the following sentence: *The goal of this paper is to ...***
    1. The goal of this paper is to analyse the hyperfeminine cyberdeck movement as a contemporary form of tech reclamation, contrasting it with the original 80s/90s cyberdeck culture, and examining it through the lens of maker culture and feminist HCI theory.*
2. **Reformulate 2. as a question.**
    1. How does the contemporary hyperfeminine cyberdeck movement function as a counter-response to exclusionary structures in mainstream tech culture?

#### III. Define your methodology:

1. **Describe in one sentence how you are planning to answer the question formulated in II.2. by finishing the sentence: *I answer the research question...***
    1. **I answer the research question** through a descriptive analysis combining a historical comparison of cyberdeck cultures (80s/90s vs. today) with an examination of contemporary online community content, framed by maker culture and feminist HCI literature.

# 3 Possible Directions:

**Direction 1 – Der historische Vergleich** *(Fokus: dann vs. heute – zwei Cyberdeck-Momente)*

**I.**

1. I am writing about the contrast between the original 80s/90s cyberdeck culture and the contemporary hyperfeminine cyberdeck movement...
2. ...because I want to show how the aesthetics, community identity, and motivations behind cyberdeck-building have fundamentally shifted between these two moments...
3. ...in order to understand what this shift reveals about changing dynamics of participation and identity in DIY tech culture.

**II.**

1. The goal of this paper is to descriptively analyse and compare two distinct moments of cyberdeck culture, the 80s/90s hacker subculture and the contemporary hyperfeminine online movement, through the lens of maker culture theory.
2. How do the aesthetics, community identity, and cultural motivations of the contemporary hyperfeminine cyberdeck movement differ from those of the original 80s/90s cyberdeck culture?

**III.**
I answer the research question through a comparative descriptive analysis of both cyberdeck movements, drawing on cyberpunk and hacker culture literature for the historical context and publicly available online community content for the contemporary movement.

---

**Direction 2 – Big Tech & Zeitkontext** *(Fokus: warum jetzt -> AI, Big Tech, und die Gegenbewegung)*

**I.**

1. I am writing about the hyperfeminine cyberdeck movement as a contemporary DIY practice emerging alongside the growing exclusivity of mainstream tech culture...
2. ...because I want to describe how this community characterizes itself, its practices, and its relationship to dominant tech structures...
3. ...in order to understand how aesthetic and DIY-driven communities position themselves within a broader landscape of techno-elitism and AI-driven industry shifts.

**II.**

1. The goal of this paper is to descriptively characterize the hyperfeminine cyberdeck movement and examine how its participants articulate their relationship to mainstream tech culture, contextualized within current developments in Big Tech and AI.
2. How do participants in the contemporary hyperfeminine cyberdeck movement characterize their practice and articulate their relationship to mainstream tech culture?

**III.**
I answer the research question through a descriptive analysis of publicly available online community content – including videos, tutorials, and community discussions – framed by maker culture and feminist HCI literature.

---

**Direction 3 – Style als Methode** *(Fokus: Ästhetik selbst als das Werkzeug der Zugänglichkeit)*

**I.**

1. I am writing about hyperfeminine aesthetics as a defining characteristic of the contemporary cyberdeck movement, using online community content as a case study...
2. ...because I want to describe what aesthetic conventions this community employs and how participants talk about style in relation to their DIY practice...
3. ...in order to understand how aesthetic identity functions within maker culture and what it signals about who this community is for.

**II.**

1. The goal of this paper is to descriptively analyse the aesthetic conventions of the hyperfeminine cyberdeck community and examine how style functions as part of its subcultural identity, drawing on feminist HCI and maker culture theory.
2. What aesthetic conventions characterize the contemporary hyperfeminine cyberdeck community, and how do participants articulate the relationship between style and DIY tech practice?

**III.**
I answer the research question through a descriptive analysis of aesthetic patterns in hyperfeminine cyberdeck community content, examined through a feminist HCI framework and supported by maker culture literature.
