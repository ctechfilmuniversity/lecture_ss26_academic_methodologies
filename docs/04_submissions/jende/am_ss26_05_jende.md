<br>

During my research on invisible image watermarking of GenAI output, I encountered several well-rounded and thorough literature reviews investigating the current state of the art in invisible image watermarking for generative AI. Therefore, I do not see much gain in conducting another literature review on the general topic itself.  

Instead, I want to investigate a question that repeatedly emerged throughout my research: Why is invisible image watermarking of GenAI imagery so challenging to apply widely in practice, despite active research, ongoing testing, and the urgent need for regulation regarding the recognizability of GenAI output?  

To address this question in my paper, I plan to first provide the reader with a very brief overview of the latest, most common invisible watermarking techniques. In the main part of the paper, I want to review literature that tests the robustness and durability of these watermarking methods on diffusion models in order to identify their major weaknesses and limitations, and to better understand why real-world implementation remains so difficult.  

Optional:  
Eventually, I want to frame this discussion within the context of small artists and companies. The goal is to provide an accessible overview of the most common invisible image watermarking techniques and their challenges, while also offering a framework that could help visual creators to orient themselves when developing their own invisible watermarking approaches to protect their work from theft.
