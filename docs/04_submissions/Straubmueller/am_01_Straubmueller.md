#### *01 - Real-Time Diffusion*

What interests me about real-time diffusion is the pattern recognition of AI in live input. Especially the combination of real-time inputs like drawings or video with TouchDesigner and Stable Diffusion/Dream Diffusion. The AI output is potentially reshaping the way we see and perceive abstract shapes we draw or things surrounding us. This could be an interesting interplay that creates a new kind of interaction or dialogue between the viewer and the AI.

The topic combines technical innovation with artistic use. The technical development is progressing quickly, which makes it possible to use such technology in various use cases, such as interactive installations, VJing, or even design workflows like brainstorming.

Possible questions:

- How does real-time feedback influence creativity?
- What other use cases are there?
- Are those use cases solely artistic, or also useful for design processes?
- What other inputs could be used for real-time diffusion?
- Is pattern recognition in abstract art changing the way we perceive it?

--- 

#### *02 - AI Development in Animation*

I find it interesting how different approaches to animation, like classic 3D animation and motion capturing, are all being transformed with the help of AI. In addition to this, there are new forms emerging, such as trajectory drawing onto images to determine how the displayed image or person should move. This new form of animating with the help of ComfyUI is fascinating and only a glimpse of how industry workflows could change.

This topic also includes technical and artistic dimensions. AI is being integrated into animation pipelines, for example for character animation, but also for style transfer and motion capturing. These developments influence not only how animations are created but also who can create them.

Possible questions:

- How will the role of animators change?
- Does it enhance or limit creativity?
- How are production pipelines changing?
- Are there other developments for animations?
- What technique makes sense for which use case?

---
  
  #### *03 - AI in Music Recommendations / Radio*

This topic interests me since I often dislike my personal music recommendations and therefore want to know how the general system works and how it is shaped by new developments like emotion recognition and AI. Furthermore, I’m also interested in the influences these recommendations have on users’ tastes and listening habits.

This topic lies at the intersection of technology, user experience, and culture. The systems are based on collaborative filtering and content‑based analysis, and they continuously learn from user behavior. While they increase convenience, they may also limit exposure to new or diverse music.

Possible questions:

- What other data could be the source of recommendations?
- Would it be better to analyze the song than the user or the filter bubble?
- Would users allow having their emotions tracked for song recommendations?
- How much control do users actually have over their music choices?
- How do recommendation algorithms impact diversity and discovery in music consumption?
