### Gamification

It is an interesting topic to begin with. Technically Games are something we do just for fun and don’t have a greater purpose. So engaging with them is inherently fun for us to begin with. We can take mechanics from gaming like progress bar, stat progressions, track them to our workouts and get extra motivated to increase. This gives an additional motivator to do something that is good for us.
There is of course a negative side to that, companies can use gamification strategies to abuse psychological effects and make us buy just one more thing, fill up the progress bar to get a discount next time etc. 



Possible Topics:
Ethical Gamification
Negative sides of Gamification

(How) can we trick ourselves into doing what's good for us through gamification? 
Have videogames taught companies how to manipulate us?

### Interactive XR installation and their advantages

My Bachelor Project was an interactive XR installation in which I explored advantages it had in comparison to a physical installation and I always wanted to find out more regarding the academic research done on this subject. 
The focus here would be on conveying information and doing it in an engaging manner. 

How is a Mixed Reality installation better than a real one?
When do we actually need XR in our Museums?
For what should I  use new technologies and what can stay as it is in the context of exhibitions?

### The ethical implications of AI

In Academic Reading 1 I read “The Creativity Code” by Marcus Du Sautoy. I read the entire book and I felt he didn’t talk about any ethical implications. He was very excited about the progress but with little regard to the downside. I would have liked to have more input regarding negative aspects of AI because I feel like they are dominating public discussion. 

In general I feel like we have different bubbles who have drastically different opinions on different types of AI and don’t really have a common moral or ethical frame regarding AI use. 

In this context im interested between the interplay of AI and creativity and wouldn’t focus on using it as an alternative search engine, an image editing tool or a coding assistant. 

Can we morally use AI?
Will creatives replace themselves with AI?
How does AI impact the creative industry?
