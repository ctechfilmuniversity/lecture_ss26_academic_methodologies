### Task 04.01 - Search Strategy

Describe your strategy to find and collect relevant literature for your topic(s). 

*Submission*: Add references and a description of their relevance. You must use whole sentences.

<br>

**Submission:**

*keywords research:*<br>
As a starting point for my general research on a topic, I write down keywords that could lead me to interesting sources and papers. Since I often can't yet shape my ideas clearly or situate them within the academic field at this stage, I search for these keywords in Google Scholar to gather inspiration from existing research. Usually I try different combinations or additions of the keywords and sometimes add academic fields such as "sociocultural," "psychological," and "computational" to guide my search in specific directions.

*tools:* <br>
Google Scholar has proven to be the main and most intuitive tool that helps me get an overview and gain insight into the subject.
Beside Google Scholar I also use Claude.ai as an supporting tool for first researches and overviews, but with the knowledge that the sources of course must be verified for reliability. Sometimes it helps me when I feel like the topic seems to be very niche. Then it can offer me a direction for further reseaches.

With other databases like MIT Press or Leonardo Magazine I am still very new. I probably will include it for further researches.

*related work:* <br>
If I found an available paper which matches my interesst on a high level I check the source references for related work. In my experience, it gives you immediate access to the research community focused on the topic and opens up further avenues of exploration beyond that. 

*collecting papers:* <br>
Previously I have simply downloaded the papers that particularly interested me and saved them in a folder where I could then read through them. For the research of this class I use Zotero to actively organize them.
