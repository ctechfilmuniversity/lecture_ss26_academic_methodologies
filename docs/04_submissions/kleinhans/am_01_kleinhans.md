### Task 01.03 - Topic Selection

**Topic 1** *AI and politics / Digital Colonialism*

Due to the global political developments and dynamics of recent years, I have come to view the use of artificial intelligence with increasing skepticism. Not in terms of its use by itself, but in terms of how it is accessed and utilized in our real world, which is shaped by power dynamics, inequalities, marginalization, and oppression. 
I am interested in how AI functions as a mirror of society, and on the other hand, AI as a tool of political instrumentalization and how it is actively used to reproduce systemic oppression, enforce right-wing agendas, and consolidate power by hegemonic actors (e.g. the USA).

Further characteristics are geopolitical and historical backgrounds about colonialism, economic aspects (e.g. how tech corporations exploit and profit from colonial structures (data extraction from Global South, labor exploitation) and how tech companies directly benefit from and actively perpetuate colonial extractive practices in the digital age) and environmental effects.

leading questions could be: 
* where is AI already used for political concerns? 
* How do hegemonic powers (USA) use AI to maintain geopolitical dominance?
* Who bears the environmental and social costs of AI infrastructure, and why are these externalities invisible/normalized?(e.g. data centers in south america, water consumption + pollution)
* who shapes ethnical rules for AI models?
* How do tech companies profit from and actively perpetuate colonial extractive practices (data mining, cheap labor, resource extraction)?
* will be AI be accessible for everyone in the future?


**Topic 2** *Movement, Embodied Healing & Creative Expression (Art Therapy, Emotion Release)*

During my last project I focused on the question "how can creative technologies make the body feel", adressing the rising bodily disconnection in our digital age. I am interested in how movement and embodied expression function as therapeutic and transformative practices. Especially how the creative approach of technologies of media art can be used to develope systems that could be used for therapetic approaches e.g. how physical movement allows us to process, release, and transform emotional and traumatic memories within the body.

That topic would include somatic/embodied therapy (e.g. dance/ movement therapy), psychology & neuroscience (nervous system) and spiritual practices. Critical perspectives could be the accessibility of those systems or the fine line between genuine healing and spirituality washing. Also feminist & decolonial perspectives would be a very interessting research topic, like embodied knowledge from marginalized communities; reclaiming indigenous and non-Western healing practices.

leading questions could be: 
* How can creative technologies support healing practices? Where is technology helpful and where not?
* How does real-time feedback from technology change embodied experience?
* How can such a system be designed? 
* Who gets access to those systems? 
* How do marginalized communities (BIPOC, LGBTQ+, disabled, trauma survivors) create and reclaim embodied healing practices?


**Topic 3** *Real-time data processing in Theater and Live performance*

Since I have always been enthusiastic about theater and stage design, and especially fascinated by full sensory productions such as operas. My general interest lies in the interaction of performers, stage, and light in real time. I find it particularly interesting how live performance is perceived and how real-time data processing transforms the relationship between performers, stage design, and sensory experience, enabling them to become responsive, live agents that react to and shape the performance in the moment.

leading questions could be:
* How can technology be used to enrich the performance/ stage design?
* Which real-time data inputs (e.g., performer movement, biometric data) can be used for stage design?
* How can human intuition be combined with algorithmic real-time environments?
